Reported Rapes in NYC: Trends To Be Aware of in 2026

Most cases happened in The Bronx and inside a residence, according to NYPD data

By Isadora Varejão | June 28, 2026

A frequent travel destination among international and domestic tourists, New York City has everything going for it in the summer of 2026: World Cup watch parties, a socialist-democratic mayor delivering on his campaign promises, and a 6'2" king anointed by basketball fans. Between the viral pizzerias, free outdoor activities, and Broadway shows, tourists will have plenty to do.

But for a woman traveling solo, deciding where to go, with whom, and when might involve more research and risk assessment.

Research says that one in five women in the U.S. will experience completed or attempted rape during their lifetime. Thanks to the NY Police Department's Complaint Data, there is information available about where and when reported rapes are happening in the city in 2026.

Where are rapes mostly being reported in NYC?

Of the 337 rapes registered with the NYPD in 2026, thirty percent were reported in The Bronx (101). Precinct 47 had the most reports in the borough. It covers the neighborhoods of Woodlawn, Wakefield, Williamsbridge, Baychester, Edenwald, Olinville, Fishbay, and Woodlawn Cemetery.

Brooklyn had the second-highest number of rape reports. Precinct 75, which encompasses East New York and Cypress Hill, received 92 complaints in 2026. That is twenty-seven percent of all cases in NYC. But at 2.8 million residents, almost twice as many as The Bronx, Brooklyn's rate of rapes per capita is dramatically lower than the first-ranked borough.

Queens had 76 reported cases, representing 23 percent. Manhattan had 53, representing 16 percent of total complaints. Staten Island comes last with five percent of all rape cases reported in 2026: 15.

Where exactly are rapes taking place?

The data shows that 75 percent of rapes reportedly happened inside or near a residence. RAINN, an anti-sexual violence organization and operator of the National Sexual Assault Hotline, may provide some additional context. Based on data from the Department of Justice, RAINN states that 68.5 percent of sexual assaults occur at or near the victim's home or their relative's home.

When have most reported rapes taken place?

Peak times vary across boroughs. While evenings seem more prevalent in Brooklyn, reported incidents frequently occurred between midnight and the early hours of the morning in Queens and Manhattan. In The Bronx and Staten Island, most reported rapes happened in the afternoon. Overall, rapes happened at all times of the day across all five boroughs.

The NYPD data doesn't tell a complete story: In 2015, the Department of Justice, through the Bureau of Justice Statistics, stated that two-thirds of completed rapes against females were not reported to the police. Shame, distrust in the criminal justice system, immigration status, fear of retaliation, and relationship to the perpetrator are common reasons.

If you or someone you know has been a victim of sexual assault, call 911 or go to a precinct to file a report and ask for an investigation to begin. You can also call the Rape, Abuse & Incest National Network (RAINN) to get advice and learn about your rights and options. Their free hotline is confidential and available 24 hours/day: 1-800-656-HOPE (4673).

Methodology: This project analyzed 2026 NYPD Complaint Data (Year To Date) sourced from NYC Open Data, focusing specifically on reported rape cases. The data was analyzed using Python's pandas library to uncover total rape reports, distribution by borough and precinct, common incident locations, and peak time periods. Crime rates per capita and percentages were calculated to account for varying borough population sizes.
